Subway Tips

This page is here to help us figure out how to navigate the subways of Tokyo.

Shiba Park

Here are the specific logistical tips for your morning journey.

1. The One App You Need

For a US tourist, Google Maps is the absolute best tool.

Example: Google Maps showing the walk to Daimon and the Asakusa Line ride.

  • Why: It tells you exactly which train car to get on (e.g., "Car 4 is best for easy transfer"), which station exit to use, and calculates the cost.
  • Pro Tip: Download "Japan Travel by NAVITIME" as a backup if you need to filter routes specifically for "Rail Pass" or "Taxi only," but for simple subway rides, Google Maps is superior.

2. Location of the Station (Daimon)

The closest entrance to the Prince Park Tower is Daimon Station, Exit A6. It is about a 9-10 minute walk from your hotel lobby.

  • Google Maps Link: Daimon Station Exit A6
  • Walking Directions: Exit the hotel, walk through Shiba Park towards the big "Gate" of Zojoji Temple. With the gate on your left, keep walking straight until you hit the main street (Hibiya-dori). The A6 exit is right near the intersection.

3. Step-by-Step Guide: Daimon to Ginza

Since you are doing this for the first time, here is the "dummy-proof" breakdown:

Step A: Payment (New for 2025)

  • The 2025 Method: As of this year, Toei Subway (which operates the Asakusa Line) has installed contactless credit card readers at many gates. If you see a reader on the ticket gate with a Visa/Mastercard contactless symbol, you can just tap your US credit card or iPhone to enter.
  • The Classic Method: If you don't see that reader, use a Suica or Pasmo card (which you can add directly to your Apple Wallet on your iPhone without buying a physical card).

Step B: The Train Ride (10 minutes)

  1. Enter Daimon Station and follow the Rose/Pink signs for the Toei Asakusa Line.
  2. Look for Platform 2 (usually) heading towards Oshiage or Narita Airport.
  3. Board the train.
  4. Get off at Higashi-Ginza Station (A11). It is only 2 stops away (Daimon -> Shimbashi -> Higashi-Ginza).

Step C: The "Underground Walk" to Ginza

  • The Secret: You do not need to get on another train. Higashi-Ginza Station and Ginza Station are effectively one giant complex connected by a long underground hallway.
  • Action: When you get off at Higashi-Ginza, look for Yellow Signs that say "Ginza Station" or "Hibiya Line / Ginza Line."
  • The Walk: You will walk straight through the underground passage (approx. 4-5 minutes). You will eventually see the department store entrances (like Mitsukoshi) on your left and right. This puts you directly in the center of Ginza without waiting at traffic lights.

Summary Plan:
Walk to Daimon A6 -> Tap phone at gate -> Asakusa Line (2 stops) -> Get off at Higashi-Ginza -> Walk underground 5 mins -> Arrive in Ginza.
