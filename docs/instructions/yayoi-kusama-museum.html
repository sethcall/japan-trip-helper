Yayoi Kusama Museum

Itinerary Instructions

Date: Dec 25 (Thursday) at 1:00 PM.

Arrival: Leave area around 12:30 PM. Arrive on time (tickets are timed).

Location: 107 Bentencho Shinjuku-ku Tokyo 162-0851.

Transfer: Taxi is most direct (~15-20 mins). Train (Oedo Line to Ushigome-yanagicho) takes longer (~30-40 mins).

Background Info

Dedicated to the avant-garde artist Yayoi Kusama, this museum opened in 2017. The five-story building, designed by Kume Sekkei, is a work of art itself with a minimalist white exterior. It houses a rotating selection of her works, including her iconic "Infinity Mirror Rooms" and polka-dot motifs. Kusama's art explores themes of infinity, repetition, and her own hallucinations.

Tips for Visitors

  • Strict Timing: Entry is strictly timed (90-minute slots). Do not be late. There is no waiting area outside, so arrive just before your slot.
  • Photography: Restricted in many areas. Usually allowed in the Infinity Room and rooftop pumpkin, but check signs. No tripods/selfie sticks.
  • Elevator: You usually take the elevator to the top and walk down (or vice versa as directed).
  • Shop: The museum shop is only accessible to ticket holders.

Exploring Afterwards

  • Soseki Park: A memorial park located on the site of author Natsume Soseki's former home. (~5 min walk) Google Maps
  • Waseda University: Stroll through the open campus of one of Japan's top universities. (~10 min walk) Google Maps
  • Anahachimangu Shrine: A historic shrine known for its horse-mounted archery festival. (~12 min walk) Google Maps
