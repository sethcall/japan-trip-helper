Background Info

Tsukiji Outer Market ("Jogai Shijo") is a district of narrow streets packed with hundreds of small wholesale and retail shops and restaurants. Although the inner wholesale market (famous for tuna auctions) moved to Toyosu in 2018, the outer market remains a vibrant hub for fresh seafood, produce, and cookware.

It is known as "Tokyo's Kitchen" and has a history dating back to the 1930s. It offers an authentic atmosphere of a bustling Japanese market.

Tips for Visitors

  • Cash is King: Many stalls only take cash.
  • Etiquette: Don't walk and eat. Buy your food and eat it at the shop front or designated area.
  • Must Eats: Tamagoyaki (sweet egg omelet on a stick), fresh sushi/sashimi, grilled scallops/oysters, and even wagyu beef skewers.
  • Shopping: Great place to buy Japanese knives, tea, and dried seafood snacks.

Exploring Afterwards

  • Tsukiji Hongwanji Temple: A unique Buddhist temple with ancient Indian architecture. (~3 min walk) Google Maps
  • Namiyoke Inari Shrine: The guardian shrine of the marketplace and its traders. (~3 min walk) Google Maps
  • Hama Rikyu Gardens: A beautiful feudal lord's garden with a seawater pond. (~12 min walk) Google Maps
