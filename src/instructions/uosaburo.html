Uosaburo (Kaiseki)

Itinerary Instructions

Date: Dec 26 (Friday) at 6:00 PM.

Location: Uosaburo 魚三楼, 3-187 Kyomachi, Fushimi-ku, Kyoto.

Transfer: Taxi (~15-20 mins) or Train (Keihan Main Line to Fushimi-Momoyama + 2 min walk).

Interpreter: Miwa Yukiko.

Background Info

Established in 1764, Uosaburo is a historic Ryotei (traditional Japanese restaurant) in the Fushimi district, famous for its sake breweries. The building itself bears bullet holes from the Battle of Toba-Fushimi (1868), a pivotal conflict in the Meiji Restoration. They serve traditional Kyoto Kaiseki cuisine using fresh seafood and the famous Fushimi groundwater.

Tips for Visitors

  • Socks: You will likely remove your shoes. Wear nice, clean socks (no holes!).
  • Bullet Holes: Ask to see the bullet holes on the lattice window at the entrance.
  • Etiquette: Kaiseki is a multi-course meal. Eat at a moderate pace. Dishes are seasonally themed and artistically presented—take a moment to appreciate the visual before eating.
