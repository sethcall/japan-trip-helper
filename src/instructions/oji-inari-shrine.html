Oji Inari-Jinja Shrine Fox Parade

Itinerary Instructions

Date: Dec 31 / Jan 1 (New Year's Eve).

Timing: Parade starts around midnight Jan 1st.

Location: Oji Inari-jinja Shrine area, Kita-ku, Tokyo.

Background Info

Based on a legend that foxes from all over the Kanto region gathered under a hackberry tree in Oji on New Year's Eve, disguised themselves as humans, and visited the shrine. Since 1993, locals have recreated this "Fox Parade" (Kitsune no Gyoretsu). Participants wear fox masks and kimono, holding lanterns, marching from Shozoku Inari Shrine to Oji Inari Shrine.

Tips for Visitors

  • Cold: It will be very cold at midnight. Dress warmly in layers!
  • Crowds: It is a popular local event. Arrive early (around 10-11 PM) to see the preparations and get a good viewing spot along the route.
  • Atmosphere: It is magical and somewhat eerie/mystical compared to the loud partying of Shibuya.
  • Masks: You can often buy fox masks at stalls nearby to join in the spirit.

Exploring Afterwards

  • Asukayama Park: A hilltop park famous for cherry blossoms and its unique monorail. (~12 min walk) Google Maps
  • Otonashi Shinsui Park: A scenic park along the old flow of the Shakujii River. (~10 min walk) Google Maps
  • Oji Shrine: One of the oldest shrines in Tokyo, known for its giant ginkgo tree. (~7 min walk) Google Maps
