Shibuya Sky

Itinerary Instructions

Date: Dec 30 (Tuesday) at 1:20 PM.

Ticket Validity: Until 1:39 PM.

Location: Shibuya Scramble Square 14th floor (ticket counter/entry).

Background Info

Shibuya Sky is a 360-degree open-air observation deck on the roof of the Shibuya Scramble Square skyscraper. It offers stunning views of the Shibuya Crossing, Tokyo Tower, Tokyo Skytree, and on clear days, Mt. Fuji. It stands about 230 meters above the ground.

Tips for Visitors

  • Lockers: You MUST put bags, hats, scarves, tripods, etc., in lockers before going to the roof (safety against wind). 100 yen coin return lockers (bring a coin!).
  • Cameras: Neck straps are usually required for cameras. Phones are okay.
  • The Edge: There's a famous glass corner for photos. Lines can be long, so go there first if you want the shot.
  • Hammocks: There are cloud hammocks to lie down and look at the sky.

Exploring Afterwards

  • Shibuya Scramble Crossing: Walk across the world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Google Maps
  • Hachiko Statue: The famous statue of the loyal dog, a popular meeting spot. Google Maps
  • Miyashita Park: A modern complex with a rooftop park, shopping, and dining. Google Maps
