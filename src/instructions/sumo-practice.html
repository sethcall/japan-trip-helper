Sumo Practice (Tatsunami-Beya)

Itinerary Instructions

Family: Edwards Family only.

Date: Dec 25 (Thursday).

Pick up: 7:00 AM at Hotel. Meeting time at stable 7:30 AM.

Location: Tatsunami-Beya, 1-16-5 Hashiba, Taito-ku, Tokyo.

Interpreter: Mami Hayashi.

Background Info

Tatsunami-Beya is a prestigious sumo stable with a history dating back to 1915. It has produced several top-ranked wrestlers (Yokozuna) in the past. Watching morning practice ("keiko") is a rare opportunity to see the intense physical and mental discipline of sumo wrestlers close up.

Tips for Visitors

  • Silence is Golden: Absolute silence is required. No whispering, talking, or phone noises. The wrestlers are extremely focused.
  • Sitting: You will likely sit on the floor (cushions). Do not stretch your legs out towards the ring (soles of feet facing the ring is rude). Sit cross-legged or with legs tucked under.
  • Photos: Usually allowed without flash (shutter sound off), but check with your guide. Video is often strictly prohibited.
  • Respect: No eating or drinking. Remove hats and sunglasses. Bow when entering/leaving if prompted.

Exploring Afterwards

  • Imado Shrine: Famous for its "matchmaking" cats and vibrant atmosphere. (~15 min walk) Google Maps
  • Sumida Park: A riverside park offering great views of the Tokyo Skytree. (~20 min walk) Google Maps
  • Senso-ji Temple: Tokyo's oldest temple in Asakusa, a 15-20 minute walk away. (~22 min walk) Google Maps
