General Travel Tips

Japan 2025 - Tokyo

Before You Go

Money & Payments

  • Cash is king: Smaller restaurants, izakayas, and local vendors often prefer cash.
  • ATMs: Use 7-Eleven, Lawson, & FamilyMart for foreign cards.
  • Currency: Japanese Yen (Â¥). Bring some for taxis and temple donations.

Tipping Etiquette

  • No tipping: It's not customary and can be considered rude.
  • Private Guides: If tipping, use a small, clean envelope.

Cultural Norms

  • Shoes off: Remove shoes in homes, ryokans, and some temples.
  • Quiet in public: Keep conversations low on trains; avoid phone calls.
  • Punctuality: Trains and tours run exactly on time.

Transportation

Suica Card

  • Works on nearly all trains, subways, and buses.
  • Add to Apple Wallet via the U.S. Suica app for easy tap-and-go.
  • Can be used at convenience stores and vending machines.

Taxis & Ubers

  • GO and JapanTaxi: Popular local apps with English support.
  • Regular Taxis: Doors open automatically. Have destination in Japanese ready.
  • Uber: Works in larger cities but is more limited.

Dining

Fragrance Policy

  • Many high-end restaurants have an unspoken no-strong-fragrance policy to avoid interfering with delicate food aromas.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sushi, Kaiseki, Tempura, Ramen, Udon, Soba, Okonomiyaki, Takoyaki, Yakitori.
