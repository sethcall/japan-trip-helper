Monday Plan

Shibuya & Harajuku Exploration

Overview

Today is all about the vibrant energy of Shibuya and the quirky culture of Harajuku.

  • First, we will head to Shibuya Station to witness the famous Scramble Crossing and see Hachiko.
  • Then, we'll explore various stores right by the station, including the multi-story Mega Don Quijote.
  • After that, we'll make our way to the famous Takeshita Dori (Street) in Harajuku for some street food and unique shopping.

Detailed Plan

Here is a plan for your walking trip from Shibuya Station to Harajuku, including the main stops and nearby suggestions.

Trip Overview

  • Total Walking Distance: Approx. 1.9 km (27 min walking time)
  • Route: Shibuya Station → MEGA Don Quijote → Nearby Options (Parco/Loft) → Takeshita Street.
  • Full Route Map: View Walking Route on Google Maps

1. Start: Shibuya Station → Main Stop

MEGA Don Quijote Shibuya Honten
The biggest Don Quijote in Tokyo, located about a 5-minute walk from Shibuya Station. It is open 24 hours and sells everything from souvenirs and cosmetics to electronics and groceries across multiple floors.

  • Highlights: Massive variety, tax-free shopping, fresh food section in the basement.
  • Location: Udagawacho area (Center-gai).

2. Very Nearby Options (2-3 min walk)

Since you are already in Udagawacho, these two popular spots are just around the corner from Don Quijote:

Shibuya Parco
A completely renovated, modern fashion complex.

  • Why go: The 6th floor is a pop-culture haven, featuring Nintendo Tokyo, the Pokemon Center Shibuya, and the Capcom Store.
  • Vibe: Trendy, high-end, and geek-culture friendly.

Shibuya Loft
A massive store dedicated to high-quality lifestyle goods.

  • Why go: Incredible selection of stationery, beauty products, and unique Japanese household items.
  • Vibe: Sophisticated, organized, and great for gifts.

3. The Walk to Harajuku

From the Udagawacho area, walk north towards Harajuku (approx. 15-20 minutes). You can walk along Koen-dori Street (passing Yoyogi Park) or Meiji-dori Avenue. Both will lead you towards Harajuku Station and the entrance to Takeshita Street.

4. Final Stop: Popular Store on Takeshita Street

Totti Candy Factory
One of the most viral and recognizable shops on Takeshita Street.

  • Famous for: Giant, multi-colored "Harajuku Rainbow" cotton candy that is larger than your head.
  • Note: It gets very crowded, but it is a quintessential Harajuku experience.

Alternative Suggestion:

Marion Crêpes Takeshita Street
The longest-running crepe shop on the street (since 1976).

  • Famous for: A huge variety of Japanese-style crepes filled with fruit, ice cream, and cheesecake.

Dessert & Coffee Guide Details

Near Don Quijote & Shibuya (The Start)

Roasted COFFEE LABORATORY

Located in the Jinnan area (about a 5-minute walk from Don Quijote), this is a perfect pit stop on your way north.

  • Best for: Coffee enthusiasts and quick bites.
  • Highlights: They roast their own beans and offer delicious coffee pairings like thick-sliced honey butter toast and hot sandwiches.
  • Map: View on Google Maps

We were in need of coffee, so finding this spot that wasn’t crowded or didn't have a long queue was a relief. It was even better when I discovered they offered gluten-free muffins and cookies. As someone who typically follows a gluten-free and lactose-free diet for health reasons (though I don’t feel the need to stick to it while on vacation), it was a pleasant surprise. I immediately decided to try the raspberry muffin\!

The only drawback is that it is a café without seating. While there are steel bars available to sit on, they are not intended for longer (or comfortable) seating. This design choice is likely intentional, and that's perfectly fine.”

On the Walk (Approaching Harajuku)

THE MATCHA TOKYO HARAJUKU

A very trendy spot dedicated to 100% organic matcha. It is located just off the main street as you get closer to Harajuku.

  • Best for: Matcha lovers and Instagram-worthy drinks.
  • Highlights: Try their Matcha Latte (often with brown sugar) or Matcha Soft Serve. You can watch them whisk the matcha right in front of you.
  • Map: View on Google Maps

Colombin Harajuku

A historic French-style confectionery shop that has been in Harajuku for decades.

  • Best for: Classic Western-style desserts.
  • Highlights: Famous for their "Harajuku Roll" cake and honey produced from bees kept on rooftops in Harajuku\!
  • Map: View on Google Maps

Near Takeshita Street (The Destination)

NOA COFFEE

Located right at the entrance of Takeshita Street (Harajuku Station side), making it an easy meeting point or rest stop before hitting the crowds.

  • Best for: Waffles and a classic coffee shop feel.
  • Highlights: Their waffles are the signature dish here. They also have a "Matcha Salon" section if you are craving Japanese sweets.
  • Map: View on Google Maps

We had the regular matcha latte and the matcha granita. The matcha latte was exactly what you would expect, good quality, decent sweetness and decent amount of matcha. The granita was the big surprise: very sweet but the amount of matcha was like an atom bomb on our tounges. This was very unexpected but we really liked it. Will come back for another granita, but not for the people that aren't used to a very strong matcha taste.”

Sadaharu AOKI

For a more high-end experience, this patisserie by a world-renowned Japanese chef offers exquisite matcha-infused pastries.

  • Best for: Gourmet souvenirs and sophisticated sweets.
  • Highlights: The Matcha Macarons and Matcha Eclairs are widely considered some of the best in Tokyo.
